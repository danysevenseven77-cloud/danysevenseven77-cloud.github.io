Explore Different Martial Arts

Every martial art has a different purpose. Some focus on striking, some focuse on grappling, and others are made for self-defense or sport competition. Learnk about each style below to see which one fits you best.

Martial Arts Styles

Learn about some of the most popular martial arts practiced today.

Karate

Karate is a Japanese martial art that focuses on powerful punches, kicks, blocks, and discipline. It teaches students how to defend themselves while also improving focus, balance, and respect. Karate is usually practiced through forms, sparring, and basic striking drills.

Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu

Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu, or BJJ, is a grappling martial art that focuses on ground fighting, submissions, and controlling an opponent. Instead of relying on size or strength, BJJ uses technique, leverage, and body positioning. It is very useful for self-defense and mixed martial arts.

Taekwondo

Taekwondo is a Korean martial art known for its fast, high, and spinning kicks. It helps build flexibility, speed, balance, and confidence. Taekwondo also includes forms, sparring, and self-defense techniques, making it both a sport and a traditional martial art.

Muay Thai

Muay Thai is a striking martial art from Thailand known as the “Art of Eight Limbs.” It uses punches, kicks, elbows, and knees to attack and defend. Muay Thai training builds strong conditioning, toughness, timing, and powerful striking skills.

Judo

Judo is a Japanese grappling martial art that focuses on throws, trips, pins, and controlling an opponent. Instead of punching or kicking, Judo teaches students how to use balance, movement, and leverage. It is also an Olympic sport and is great for building strength and coordination.

Krav Maga

Krav Maga is a modern self-defense system developed for real-life situations. It focuses on simple, direct techniques to escape danger quickly. Krav Maga includes strikes, defenses against grabs, and practical skills for staying safe in stressful situations.
